Banker's Algorithm

The Banker's Algorithm is a resource allocation and deadlock avoidance algorithm developed by Edsger Dijkstra. It's designed to test the safety of allocating resources to processes without causing a deadlock. The algorithm considers the current available resources, the maximum resources each process may need, and the current allocation of resources to processes.
